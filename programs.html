Our Program Areas

Eight comprehensive program categories addressing the root causes through systematic, evidence-based interventions.

Program Description Core Objectives
1: Food Security & Livelihoods
  • Builds household resilience and expands livelihood options in South Sudan.
  • Supports sustainable agricultural systems.

  • Humanitarian relief in Uganda: food and non-food items for displaced populations.
  • Promotes food security, human dignity, and resilience in crisis-affected communities.
 Nutrition-Sensitive Agriculture

Household gardens and cultivation of nutrient-rich crops (e.g., vegetables and legumes) to improve nutrition outcomes and support child health.
2: Health & Nutrition
  • Focuses on community health and nutrition for women, adolescent girls, and children under five and enhances healthcare systems in South Sudan.

  • In partnership with Rotary Clubs and the South Sudanese women community in Australia, the Life of Service Foundation (LSF) donated critical medical equipment to Bor State Hospital in Jonglei State, South Sudan. This targeted intervention enhanced the hospital's capacity to deliver essential health services, with a particular focus on improving maternal and child health outcomes.
 Maternal & Child Health:

Delivery of antenatal and postnatal care, immunization services, child growth monitoring, and maternal nutrition support.


Girl's Support Network (GSN):

Menstrual hygiene management, reproductive health awareness, psychosocial support, and peer mentorship.


Nutrition Education:

Community-led behaviour change campaigns on breastfeeding, balanced diets, and infant feeding practices especially in displacement settings.


Healthcare Access:

Expansion of essential services through mobile clinics, trained community health workers, and equipped health facilities in hard-to-reach areas.


Health Policy Advocacy:

Engagement with the Ministry of Health and local stakeholders to close service gaps and advocate for inclusive public health systems.

3: Education & Capacity Building
  • LSF views education as a fundamental human right and a cornerstone of sustainable development. Its inclusive, rights-based approach ensures that vulnerable children, youth, and adults gain equitable access to quality learning and life-enhancing opportunities.

  • Advancing Inclusive Education for Vulnerable Children From November 2016 to 2025, the Life of Service Foundation (LSF), in partnership with Rotary Clubs in Australia, provided scholarships and educational materials to vulnerable children across underserved communities at Boston secondary school and Matopeni slum in Nairobi, kenya. The initiative promoted inclusive and equitable quality education, contributing to Sustainable Development Goal 4 (Quality Education) by reducing barriers to access, supporting learning continuity, and fostering long-term empowerment for marginalized children.
 Access to Education in Crisis- Affected Areas:

Safe learning spaces, school supplies, rehabilitated infrastructure, and trained teachers in post-conflict communities with a strong emphasis on girls' retention and success.


Street-to-School Scholarship Initiative:

A holistic education and protection program targeting street-involved and orphaned children, providing scholarships, psychosocial support, and mentorship to advance equity and inclusion.


Refugee Child Protection & Resilience Program :

In partnership with local actors and UNHCR, this initiative provides community-based education access, psychosocial support, birth registration, and child protection for unaccompanied and separated refugee children.


Girl's Support Network (GSN):

Supporting adolescent girls in boarding schools and universities through mentorship, dignity kits, tutoring, and scholarships addressing social, psychological, and academic barriers.


Teacher Training & Education System Strengthening:

Capacity building for educators and school administrators on inclusive pedagogy, learner engagement, and leadership to enhance education outcomes in fragile settings.


Vocational & Life Skills Training:

Practical training in tailoring, agriculture, carpentry, and entrepreneurship equips youth and adults with skills for economic independence and civic leadership.


Civic Leadership & Governance Workshops:

Workshops that foster civic responsibility, peacebuilding, and local governance engagement among youth and community leaders.


Child protection and education services in Jonglei State (2022-2023):

LSF partnered with Blessed Home for Orphans, Vagrants, Delinquents & Widows (BHOVDW) to strengthen child protection and education services in Jonglei State. Through technical and material support to the Blessed Home Orphans Primary School, LSF enhanced access to quality education and care. The partnership resulted in reaching 300 children with child rights awareness, providing essential services to

120 orphans, enabling 205 children to complete primary education, and delivering psychosocial support to 100 children and widows. Creative trauma healing activities further promoted resilience, demonstrate · the impact of locally led collaboration in fragile contexts.

4: Gender Equality & Women's Rights
  • As a women-founded and women-led organization, LSF integrates gender equality across all programming and institutional structures. Through a transformative, rights-based approach, LSF advances women's leadership, protection, and meaningful participation in peacebuilding, governance, and development.

 Women’s Leadership & Economic Empowerment:

LSF builds the capacity of women to lead in civic, economic, and governance spaces. Programs such as the Transformational Leadership Workshops in Ayod County (2023) have equipped women and girls to engage in local decision-making bodies and peace forums.


Gender-Based Violence (GBV) Prevention & Response:

Community-based GBV response frame­ works integrate survivor support, community education, and capacity-building for stakeholders. Interventions challenge harmful norms and strengthen local accountability systems, especially in GBV-prone contexts like Jonglei State.


Legal Literacy & Advocacy:

Through training and rights awareness campaigns, LSF promotes understanding of gender responsive legal frameworks on marriage, property rights and education empowering women and girls to claim their rights and engage in policy reform.


Women in Peace & Governance Processes :

LSF fosters women's participation in peacebuilding through tailored training in mediation, negotiation, and conflict resolution. Programs like the 2020 Young Women's Participation in Peace Processes have strengthened women's roles in formal and traditional peace platforms.
5: Water, Sanitation & Hygiene (WASH)
  • Access to safe water, adequate sanitation, and hygiene is foundational to public health, human dignity, and community resilience especially in post-conflict and disaster - prone contexts. LSF's integrated WASH programming addresses these basic needs while promoting behaviour change and gender-responsive service delivery.

 Water Supply Systems:

Construction and rehabilitation of bore ­holes, wells, and water storage systems in underserved communities reducing waterborne illnesses and the burden on women and children who walk long distances to access water.


Sanitation Infrastructure:

Installation of gender-sensitive sanitation facilities in schools, health centres, and displacement camps. LSF employs Community - Led Total Sanitation (CLTS) approaches to encourage community ownership and ensure sustainability.


Hygiene Promotion & Menstrual Health:

Community and school-based hygiene education campaigns on handwashing, water safety, and menstrual hygiene. Hygiene kits with soap, water purification tablets, and menstrual products are distributed during emergencies and recovery efforts.


Emergency WASH Response:

Deployment of lifesaving WASH services during crises. During the 2021 floods in Jonglei State, LSF delivered clean water, hygiene supplies, and supported protection of vital water points in IDP settlements.
6: Peacebuilding & Social Cohesion
  • In the context of South Sudan's fragile peace, LSF places peacebuilding and social cohesion at the core of its programming. By addressing root causes of conflict and building inclusive community structures for, dialogue, reconciliation, and economic integration, LSF supports durable peace and recovery.

 Football4Peace initiative:

LSF supported a Football4Peace initiative in partnership with Bor Football Club, engaging 32 children and youth aged 10-18 in 2021-2022, in Jonglei State. The program used football to promote peace building, social cohesion, and youth empowerment among communities affected by conflict.LSF provided equipment, mentorship, psychoso­ cial support, and coordinated local engagement to foster unity and non- violence. The initiative strengthened trust among youth, encouraged leadership, and created positive role models. LSF seeks support to scale the program to other conflict-prone areas in South Sudan.


Herders4Peace (HoPe):

A signature initiative that addresses cattle-related violence through integrated community dialogue, indigenous conflict resolution, and shared natural resource governance. HoPe empowers youth and traditional leaders to prevent violence and sustain peace in Jonglei, Greater Pibor, and Equatoria.


lntercommunal Dialogue & Reconciliation:

Facilitated conflict resolution forums bringing together elders, women, youth, and civic leaders to address ethnic tensions, post-displacement grievances, and trauma healing.


Conflict Resolution & Youth Engagement:

Youth are trained in peacebuilding, trauma-informed mediation, and local governance, enabling them to lead reconciliation initiatives and strengthen community peace infrastructure.


Peace Education & Civic Awareness :

Civic forums and educational campaigns on the Revitalized Peace Agreement (R-ARCSS) and local governance enhance transparency, participation, and trust in public institutions.


Livelihoods for Peace:

Economic reintegration support vocational training, microenterprise development, and livelihood kits for ex-combatants, youth, and women, linking peacebuilding to economic stability.


Human Rights & Social Justice Advocacy:

Advocacy on land rights, gender justice, and inclusive governance is embedded across peace programs to support systemic accountability and social equity.
7: Environmental Protection & Climate Action
  • LSF addresses the urgent challenges of climate change and environmental degradation through inclusive, community based initiatives that restore ecosystems, build resilience, and elevate local leadership.

 Women4Climate & Nature (W4CN):

A gender-responsive adaptation program training rural women in sustainable agriculture, climate risk reduction, and policy engagement. Since 2022, over 500 women have been trained and 20 demonstration farms launched in Jonglei and Lakes States.


Youth4Climate& Nature (Y4CN):

A youth-led initiative advancing climate innovation, advocacy, and knowledge exchange. Young leaders receive mentorship, technical guidance, and seed funding to drive grassroots solutions across South Sudan.


Communities4Climate&Nature (C4CN):

A community mobilization platform for Eco system restoration, including tree planting, school and community gardens, and clean-up, campaigns. Over 1,000 volunteers have engaged in local climate action activities.


Climate Resilience Projects:

In partnership with the Ministry of Environment and civil society, LSF leads projects in reforestation, agroforestry, and land conservation guided by the climate Resilient Green Economy (CRGE) framework.


Disaster Risk Reduction (DRR):

Local DRR committees, contingency planning, and responder training in flood prone regions have improved emergency preparedness and early warning systems.


Climate Education & Policy Advocacy :

Eco-clubs, media campaigns, and village forums promote environmental awareness and citizen engagement. LSF also contributes to inclusive climate policy dialogue and community representation at national levels.
8: Volunteer Engagement for Sustainable Development
  • LSF partners with international volunteer-sending programs to deploy skilled professionals in support of locally driven development across South Sudan and the region. These volunteer placements strengthen institutional capacity, advance the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs), and foster resilience in fragile and conflict-affected communities.

  • Volunteers contribute across key sectors, including health, education, gender equality, child protection, ICT, law and justice, agriculture, and public policy. Their engagement supports knowledge transfer, inclusive service delivery, and cross-cultural collaboration, driving long-term, sustain­ able development outcomes.
