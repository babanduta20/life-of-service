The Youth Participation in Peace and Development project in Ayod County

The Youth Participation in Peace and Development project in Ayod County empowered young people with leadership and conflict resolution skills to actively engage in peace­ building and governance. Through training and dialogue forums, the initiative enhanced youth inclusion in community and national decision-making, contributing to social cohesion and aligning with the UN Youth, Peace and Security agenda.

Women and Youth Workshop on Peace and Transformational Leadership

Empowering women and youth through peace and transformational leadership training.

The Presentation on the Revitalized Agreement on the Resolution

The Presentation on the Revitalized Agreement on the Resolution of the Conflict in South Sudan (R-ARCSS) held in Bor County aimed to raise community awareness and promote local ownership of the peace process. By educating stakeholders on the key provisions of the R-ARCSS, the session enhanced public understanding and engagement, contributing to grassroots support for peace implementation and long-term stability in the region.

The Mobile Clinics for Vulnerable Groups

The Mobile Clinics for Vulnerable Groups initiative in Jonglei State (Bor, Duk, and Twi East) provided life-saving healthcare services to women, children, and the elderly in underserved communities. The clinics focused on the treatment of malaria and waterborne diseases, addressing urgent health needs in fragile settings. This intervention supported improved health outcomes and aligned with humanitarian priorities for equitable access to essential services in conflict-affected areas.

Community Volunteers in Bor Town

Community Volunteers in Bor Town, Jonglei State, played a critical support role in flood prevention efforts by mobilizing resources providing tea and snacks to dyke repair workers. This grassroots initiative demonstrated strong community solidarity and local ownership in disaster risk reduction, contributing to the protection of lives and livelihoods in a highly flood-prone region.

The Practical Guide to the Constitution-Building Process training

The Practical Guide to the Constitution-Building Process training, conducted in Bor Town and the Protection of Civilians (POC) site, equipped women with the knowledge and tools to meaningfully engage in South Sudan's constitutional development. By enhancing their understanding of the constitution-building process, the training promoted women's active participation in governance and legal reform, advancing gender inclusion and democratic accountability in post-conflict state-building.

The Training to Increase Women and Girls' Participation in Community and Government

The Training to Increase Women and Girls' Participation in Community and Government, held at Dr. John Garang Memorial University, empowered head boys, head girls, and student leaders to champion gender equality and inclusive leadership. The program strengthened the capacity of young leaders to advocate for and support the active involvement of women and girls in university governance and community decision-making, contributing to long-term gender equity and inclusive development.

Women in Bor, Jonglei State

Women in Bor, Jonglei State, play a critical role in peacebuilding through mediation, advocacy, and community healing. Despite their impact, they face barriers such as exclusion from formal processes and limited resources. Supporting their inclusion is essential for achieving lasting peace and social cohesion in the region.

sensitization workshop

A sensitization workshop engaged political, traditional, and religious leaders to raise awareness on women's rights and gender-based violence (GBV). The workshop strengthened their role in advocacy, prevention, and response efforts, promoting a safer and more inclusive society for women and girls.

Young women on conflict resolution and leadership skills

Empowered young women with conflict resolution and leadership skills to actively participate in peacebuilding. The program promoted their inclusion in decision-making, supporting gender equality and sustainable peace in line with UNSCR 1325.

humanitarian assistance to internally displaced persons

LSF provided humanitarian assistance to internally displaced persons affected by conflict and flooding across Malual-Agorbaar (Total), Freedom Square, and the River Nile Displacement Center. The intervention delivered essential food and non-food items to address urgent needs, protect vulnerable populations, and support crisis-affected communities in line with humanitarian response priorities.

Women and Youth Leadership Program

The Women and Youth Leadership Program led by the Office of the Vice President (Gender and Youth Cluster) in collaboration with UNDP and Leadwise, is a flagship national initiative aimed at empowering educated but unemployed youth particularly young women through structured, paid internships.So far, 100 interns have been successfully deployed across UN agencies, NGOs, intergovernmental bodies, and the private sector. A second cohort of 100 interns has completed recruitment and interviews and is awaiting deployment.

LSF leads the Consortium for Resilient Communities South Sudan,

partnering with national NGOs to deliver locally driven climate and resilience programs. The initiative has planted over 1,000 trees, supported 25 adaptation groups, and trained 100+ youth climate ambassadors demonstrating a scalable, community-based model for impactful and equitable climate action.

Improve child protection and education in Jonglei State

In 2022-2023, the Life of Service Foundation (LSF) partnered with BHOVDW to improve child protection and education in Jonglei State. The collaboration supported 205 children to complete primary education, provided essential services to 120 orphans, raised child rights awareness among 300 children, and delivered psychosocial support to 100 children and widows. Creative trauma healing activities promoted community resilience, highlighting the impact of locally led initiatives in fragile contexts.
