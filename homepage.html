Introduction

The Life of Service Foundation (LSF) is a women-led, legally registered national NGO operating across South Sudan, Ethiopia, Uganda, and Kenya, with its headquarters in Juba, South Sudan.Established by South Sudanese-Australian humanitarian Rachel Amuor Riak, LSF brings over a decade of experience in delivering inclusive, community-driven solutions across the humanitarian, development and peace nexus.


LSF designs and implements high-impact programs in health, education, gender equality, livelihoods, climate resilience, WASH, and peacebuilding, aligned with the South Sudan NGO Act, the Sustainable Development Goals (SDGs) and international humanitarian standards.


Flagship initiatives such as Street-to-School, Youth4Climate & Nature, and Women4 Climate & Nature exemplify LSF's capacity to scale locally owned, innovative responses that prioritize the empowerment of women, youth, children, and persons with disabilities.


Governed by a gender-balanced board and supported by a strong Monitoring, Evaluation, Accountability, and Learning (MEAL framework, LSF collaborates closely with agencies, international NGOs, bilateral donors, and government stakeholders. The organization has reached over 100,000 individuals across fragile and conflict-affected contexts through programs rooted in rights-based, inclusive, and sustainable development principles.


LSF leverages the transformative power of volunteerism, grassroots leadership, and community solidarity to address poverty inequality, and exclusion. Its approach fosters local ownership, intergenerational resilience, and inclusive participation,making it a preferred partner for donors and development actors seeking impactful, locally led interventions.

Governance and Institutional Structure draft

The Life of Service Foundation (LSF) is a professionally governed, women-led national NGO with a robust institutional frame designed for transparency, scalability, and results-based programming. It is overseen by a multidisciplinary, gender-balanced Board of Directors, guided by a high-level Advisory Council, and man­ aged by a seasoned Executive Leadership Team. To ensure effective delivery and donor accountability, LSF operates through six core departments, each comprising specialized sub-units for internal Coordination.
Performance tracking, and program impact.


1. Programs & Impact Department:

Leads the design and implementation of Integrated interventions across: child Protection & Safeguarding, Education & Youth Empowerment, Gender & Social Inclusion,Livelihoods & Economic Empowerment, Climate Resilience & Environmental Action and Peacebuilding & Social Cohesion.


2. Finance & Operations Department

Ensures sound financial manage and compliance through: Budgeting & Financial Oversight ,Grants Compliance & Risk ,Management Procurement, Logistics & Asset Control and Internal Audit & Financial Accountability.


3. Monitoring, Evaluation & Capacity Build ing (MECB):

Drives data-informed decision-making and institutional learning via: Monitoring & Results Tracking ,Evaluation & Knowledge Management and Staff & Systems Capacity Development.


4. Resource Mobilization & Partner ships:

Strengthens sustainability and donor alignment through:Grant Writing & Proposal Development ,Donor Reporting & Relationship Management and Strategic Partnerships & Private Sector Engagement.


5. Communications & Advocacys:

Amplifies community voices and policy influence through: Public Relations & Media Outreach ,Digital Campaigns & Storytelling and Rights-Based Policy Advocacy & Publications .


6. Support Services:

Delivers core organizational functions, including: Human Resources & Organizational Development ,ICT & Legal Advisory and Field Operations, Security & Administration.
